Paper Airplane

Instruction Manual
Oct 2022

My Angle

First, I thought about the way that kids think and the issues that they ran into when trying to fold a paper airplane. I worked at a summer camp and many kids do not have great fine-motor skills. They also do not always know where to place their fingers. This issue is what made me think of the colored dots. I also thought about how kids are very visual. I decided that less words and more images would be better.

Then, I created various sketches of the overall design and layout. I had to go make multiple iterations before creating something functional and visually pleasing.

Finally, I used Adobe Illustrator to create the airplane images and final design. There were various new ideas that I incorporated into the final design during the creation process.

PAC Positive

Affirmation Cookies

Social Issue Project
Dec 2022

Project Description

For this project, our task was to create something that targets a social issue. Our group had to work together to research, brainstorm, problem solve to create and present a physical prototype of our solution. We were encouraged to use reused or recyclable materials. We wanted to create a product that would improve the mental health of any individual. Our main goal was to focus on the users throughout the design process. We wanted to do this by making it accessible, functional, and innovative. Throughout the project we followed the “Critical Making” Concept; Ideation, Understanding, Inspiration, Making and Sharing.

Process

1

Inspiration &
Understanding

First, we were inspired by other similar DIY projects. Then we started to brainstorm various ideas; we practiced divergent thinking by sharing all our good and bad ideas. During this process, we researched various social issues and decided to choose a social issue that is relevant to our society and resonated with us. After, we chose mental health as our targeted social issue and then, did some more research on the topic.

2

Ideation

When coming up with the final idea, all the group members built upon each other's ideas and concepts. We first came up with an idea inspired by fortune cookies. Our product would be similar but instead, the messages would be positive and the cookie recipes would be different. Instead of getting the product at a restaurant, they could be easily obtained in schools and workplaces. We used google drawings to make rough sketches of our ideas as well as improve and add to the original main concept. By the end of our session, we had a product concept with the name “PACs”; Positive Affirmation Cookies. A “PAC” is a small box that has a few cookies in it, two different positive messages and a mental health phone line. People can get them in a weekly package, larger box or individually from a dispenser. Buyers are encouraged to share positive messages with others. All additional profits go to the Canadian Mental Health Association.

3

Making

When prototyping, all the specific elements of the design involved adjusting and tinkering. We were originally going to make slots for each individual box to fit into; however, we realized that would be an inefficient use of materials as the smaller boxes were created in such a way that they did not require extra support or a structure for an organization.

Challenge

Constructing the geometric nets for the small weekly PAC boxes.

Response

We had another box as a reference but had to significantly alter the dimensions and ultimate net layout to create a sturdy structure.

Something that worked well was the use of unique paper for the packaging. The green colour theme and fun patterns create a positive and visually appealing product. Green ribbons are also used to create mental health awareness.

4

Final Product

Here are some photos of the finished product. The prototype is intended to illustrate the concept thus, all the individual weekly boxes are not present. At the end of this project, our group shared and discussed our process and prototype with the class. We engaged in a reflective conversation about the concept and potential improvements or alterations. 

Reflection

This project taught me about designing for a social issue. Having empathy for a diverse set of users is important. I also learned about designing in a group setting; it is important to make use of everyone’s best ideas and skills. The design process contributed to my evolution as a UX Designer by leading me through the various aspects of design. There is a lot that can be learned through the communication of knowledge and hands-on experience but, there is so much that can be gained when these two types of learning come together as they did during this project.

References

Ratto, Matt. “Critical Making: Conceptual and Material Studies in Technology and Social Life.” The Information Society, vol. 27, no. 4, 15 July 2011, pp. 252–260., https://doi.org/10.1080/01972243.2011.583819.

PAC Positive

Affirmation Cookies

Social Issue Project
Dec 2022

Project Description

For this group project, we were required to build an accessible box that reconfigured and “hacked” the original purpose of a box. We were allowed to choose any type of box. The design had to be creative, functional, and ergonomic. Students were encouraged to use recycled materials to create a prototype illustrating the concept.

Process

1

Inspiration &
Understanding

First, we thought of various boxes. We brainstormed ideas and decided to go with a delivery box. Then, we defined and learned about the users. We grouped them into three main categories; shippers, delivery people and receivers. Then we researched the main pain points that these users experience. The main issues with package delivery are, damaged products in shipment, lost or stolen packages and incorrect package delivery locations.

2

Ideation

We brainstormed and drew various ideas on paper and in google drawings. During this, we were able to build and form our design. Our final concept “hacked” the typical delivery box as it had handles that could also be used as backpack straps and a bright red zipper. These made the box conceptually and physically easier to use. The box had compartments and padding preventing items from breaking. The concept also included a tracker connected to an app. The tracker and app let users know the location of the box. This would help prevent lost, stolen, and mis-delivered boxes.

3

Making

During the prototyping process, something that went well was glueing the handles/backpack straps to the box as this was a fairly straightforward process.

Challenge

We used an old backpack and struggled to detach the zipper. It was also difficult to get it to work probably after it was detached.

Response

Various group members worked at detaching the zipper. We glued the zipper onto the box in a way that clearly showed the concept.

Challenge

Creating effective compartments that could be modified by the users. The slits in the cardboard compartment pieces were not the right length initially.

Response

We modified, adjusted and tested the compartments until they were successful.

4

Final Product

Here are some images of the final product. We were able to present it to the class in a “Show and Tell” format. There were great conversations about the product’s functionality as well as potential future improvements.

Reflection

This project taught me about the critical making process. In this process, people research, gather and produce ideas. Then, they collectively make a prototype. Afterward, they reflect on the design through conversations and personal thought. This cycle then continues as the project develops (Ratto 253). Throughout the project, we collectively gathered ideas, materials, and produced a prototype. We reflected on our creation before, during, and after the “Show and Tell” period. This process contributed to my growth as a designer by allowing me to gain more hands-on design experience. 

References

Ratto, Matt. “Critical Making: Conceptual and Material Studies in Technology and Social Life.” The Information Society, vol. 27, no. 4, 15 July 2011, pp. 252–260., https://doi.org/10.1080/01972243.2011.583819.
